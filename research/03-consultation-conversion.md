## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (15 lines)

The evidence strongly supports ONE form with TWO entry points, not two separate forms.
Wisetack's canonical playbook uses the exact same prequal link for both: dropped in ads/website
pre-consultation, and sent by a rep post-consultation via SMS. This matches how HVAC/roofing
reps operate -- they text a financing link at the kitchen table and the customer applies on
their own phone. Duplicating the form adds maintenance cost and splits your data.

Lead response speed is the single most leveraged variable: the 5-minute rule is real and
verified (MIT/InsideSales, 2011 HBR study, 2.24M leads), not vendor spin. Sub-5-minute
response produces 21x qualification lift vs. 30 minutes, 100x contact rate vs. 30 minutes.

Soft-pull prequalification delivered before the visit (via SMS link) increases conversion 26%
and ticket size 30% (Wisetack internal data, 2023-2024). The psychology: customer arrives
knowing their budget ceiling -- objection already removed before the rep opens their mouth.

Small deposit ($25-50 refundable) cuts no-show rate ~57-70% (OpenTable/Tock data, directly
applicable to any booked-consultation service). Use it for UltraWash consultation bookings.

For 0% financing specifically: the HVAC industry shows close rate lifts from 38% to 49% when
financing is offered. Sunbit processed $1B+ in auto services at 90%+ approval rate. BNPL
lifts average order value 30-85% depending on the platform (Affirm/Klarna data). These numbers
are directly portable to a premium detailing/protection flow.

ONE FORM / TWO ENTRY POINTS: Yes. Customer hits it from the ad (self-serve). Rep texts the
same link post-walk (rep-assisted). Same logic, same brand experience, same data funnel.

## SECTION 2: Two-Sided Flow -- How High-Ticket Services Run Quote-to-Finance

### 2.1 The Standard Home-Service Rep-Assisted Model

The proven pattern from Wisetack, Hearth, GreenSky, Synchrony for home improvement:

```
Step 1: Customer books online (or rep books after inbound call)
Step 2: Rep sends prequal link via SMS BEFORE the appointment
        -- customer sees their budget ceiling before the visit
Step 3: In-home / in-shop consultation + quote
Step 4: Rep sends formal financing application link via SMS on the spot
        -- customer applies on their own phone in < 60 seconds
Step 5: Approval in 30 seconds (Wisetack/Sunbit). Customer picks term.
Step 6: Job confirmed. Deposit (if any) deducted. Work begins.
```

**Key insight:** The rep never handles a paper application or tablet input. They confirm the
phone number and tap "Send." Customer self-serves on their own device. This is the standard
across Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, FieldPulse integrations with Wisetack.

### 2.2 Platform Specifics

| Platform | Fit | Key Stat | Notes |
|----------|-----|----------|-------|
| **Wisetack** | Home services, some auto | $500-25K, 3.9% merchant fee, 80%+ approval | Prequal + full app. Best for service businesses. No subscription. |
| **Sunbit** | Automotive (OEM + independent) | $60-10K, 90% approval, 30-sec decision | Designed for service drive / shop counter. Already in 6K+ dealerships. |
| **GreenSky** | Home improvement (larger tickets) | $0-100K, 0-26.6% dealer fee | Goldman-owned. Better for $5K+ jobs. More friction. |
| **Hearth** | Contractors, tablet-first | No setup fee, subscription model | App-first, landscape iPad mode. Good for in-home close. |
| **Affirm** | Broad retail + services | Avg order $276, 85% AOV lift on Affirm vs cash | Better known to consumers. Higher avg order. |

**UltraWash application:** Sunbit for in-shop (if you have a bay), Wisetack for mobile/home
services. Both support the same send-link-via-SMS rep-assisted flow.

### 2.3 Salesperson-Facing Tool Pattern

Wisetack's documented workflow for the rep:
1. Pull up job in mobile app (Housecall Pro, Jobber, or direct Wisetack merchant app)
2. Confirm customer phone number
3. Tap "Send Financing Options" -- SMS fires in <5 seconds
4. Customer applies on their own device while rep answers questions
5. Rep receives notification on approval (often before customer looks up)

Hearth adds: full estimate, contract, and financing in one SMS thread. Rep never needs paper.

## SECTION 3: Prequalification Psychology -- Soft-Pull "60 Seconds, No Impact"

| Metric | Data | Source |
|--------|------|--------|
| Prequal vs no-prequal: conversion lift | +26% more likely to move forward | Wisetack (comparisons within their platform, 2023-2024 data) |
| Prequal vs no-prequal: spend lift | +30% more per transaction | Wisetack internal study |
| Send prequal BEFORE appointment | Industry best practice -- answers "what can I afford?" before objection arises | Wisetack prequal link documentation |
| BNPL overall AOV lift (Affirm) | 85% higher AOV vs cash | Affirm merchant data |
| BNPL overall AOV lift (Klarna) | 45-73% higher AOV | Klarna merchant data |
| BNPL conversion lift (RBC research) | +20-30% conversion, +30-50% ticket size | RBC Capital Markets, McKinsey BNPL report |
| Wisetack: financed jobs vs average | 4.5x larger ($4,500 vs $1,000 avg) | Wisetack Impact of Financing report, 2024 |
| Wisetack: merchants who won jobs via financing | 87% of enrolled merchants | Wisetack 2024 report |

**Key mechanism:** "No impact to credit" removes the #1 objection to applying. 60-second soft
pull is psychologically equivalent to a free trial -- near-zero cost to check. Presenting it as
"see what you qualify for, takes 60 seconds, nothing on your credit" is the exact framing that
Wisetack, Sunbit, and Hearth all use. It works.

**Caution on data:** Wisetack's 26%/30% figures are from their own platform -- no independent
peer-reviewed confirmation. But the direction is consistent with every BNPL study and the
behavioral economics literature on pre-commitment. Treat as directionally sound.

## SECTION 4: Deposit-to-Book vs Free Consultation

| Context | No-Show Rate (no deposit) | No-Show Rate (with deposit) | Source |
|---------|--------------------------|----------------------------|--------|
| Restaurant reservations | ~10-15% | ~1.7% (Tock) | Tock/Exploretock |
| Restaurant (OpenTable deposit model) | Baseline | -57% no-shows | OpenTable |
| Med spa consultations | 30-60% ghost rate after booking | Deposit cuts significantly | Aesthetics Junkie, RunMedSpa |
| General service booking | Industry-wide ~20-25% no-show | Deposit: 5-15% no-show | Various, UNVERIFIED at exact % |

**Volume concern:** Med spa data shows low-commitment prospects (from discount ads) have the
worst show rates. Requiring a deposit filters them naturally -- this is a feature, not a bug,
for a premium-positioning brand like NEBO/UltraWash.

**Refundable vs non-refundable:** For a consultation-based service (not the job itself),
refundable is the safe call. Reduces cancellation before the visit without creating consumer
protection risk or negative reviews.

**UltraWash number:** $25-50 refundable. At current lead cost, filtering 1 no-show per 10
bookings pays for the entire system.

## SECTION 7: Highest-Leverage Levers (What Financing Platforms Publish)

Synthesized from Wisetack, Sunbit, GreenSky, Hearth published playbooks:

1. **Send prequal link before the appointment** -- removes the "I can't afford it" objection
   before it's raised. Wisetack: +26% close rate, +30% ticket size.

2. **Offer financing at the quote, not at the close** -- present it as "here's how people
   typically pay for this" not "before you say no, here's a payment plan." Reframes entirely.

3. **90%+ approval messaging** -- tell the customer before they apply. "Most people qualify."
   Removes fear of rejection as an objection.

4. **SMS link, not a web form** -- customer applies on their own phone, their own UI, their
   own pace. Rep presence removes anxiety. 30-second approval.

5. **Integrate into your booking confirmation** -- Wisetack's #1 underused feature: prequal
   link in appointment reminder SMS/email. Customer sees their limit before they arrive.

6. **Same-day close is 3x more likely than follow-up close** -- HVAC sales data. Emotion and
   commitment are highest at end of consultation. Send the link right then.

## SECTION 8: ONE Form / TWO Entry Points -- The Verdict

**Question:** Should UltraWash have one form serving both (a) customer self-serve from ad
and (b) rep sends post-consultation to finalize -- or two separate forms?

**Answer: ONE FORM, TWO ENTRY POINTS. This is industry standard.**

**Evidence:**
- Wisetack's entire prequal architecture is built on a single shareable link used across ads,
  email, website, SMS at-quote, and appointment reminders -- same link, same form.
- Hearth is the same: one link sent from the app that serves both marketing and rep-close.
- Sunbit's POS: same tablet/phone app whether customer walks in (self-serve) or rep initiates.
- The flow is contextually identical -- customer fills out their own form on their device.
  The only variable is WHEN (pre-consultation vs post-consultation) and WHO sends it (ad
  click vs rep SMS).

**Two separate forms would:**
- Double your maintenance surface
- Split conversion data (can't A/B test properly)
- Create brand inconsistency
- Confuse the rep about which link to send

**How to handle the two contexts with one form:**
- Use UTM parameters or a query string to track source (ad vs rep-sent)
- Optionally: pre-populate "How did you hear about us?" based on entry point
- The submission logic is identical; reporting splits by source tag

**UltraWash recommendation:**
Build one prequal flow at `/financing` or `/apply`. Drop it in ads. Rep texts the same URL
post-consultation. Tag the source with `?ref=rep` vs `?ref=ad`. Done.
